### Source guide
This document outlines a sales strategy for a financial service focused on capitalising on corporate insider trading, specifically the disclosed purchases made by high-level executives like CFOs and VPs. The service offers a single, all-inclusive subscription at $100 per month, providing users with high-conviction trade ideas designed for opportunistic swing trades, averaging two months in duration. The core rationale is that insiders generally only buy shares when they believe the price will increase, and the system filters this data by prioritising cluster buying and large-dollar-value transactions to distinguish genuine signals from routine activity. Additionally, the service provides a separate managed account service for clients who require assistance with trade execution and management.

#### 3) The Logic Behind High-Conviction Corporate Trades ü§î

The foundation of our strategy rests on a simple truth: **insiders know more than outsiders**.

"Insider trading" involves trading in company stock by the company's officers, directors, or influential shareholders (those owning more than 10% of the stock). These individuals are allowed to buy and sell shares of their own stock, provided they release this information in a timely manner via an SEC filing (Form 4).

While there are many reasons why an insider might **sell** shares‚Äîsuch as paying bills, buying a house, diversifying their holdings, or receiving shares as compensation‚Äîthere is typically **only one reason why an insider will buy shares in their company:** they believe in the company and think the share value is going to go up over time.

By carefully monitoring this publicly available buying activity, focusing specifically on **Corporate Insiders** [Conversation History], we pinpoint opportunities based on:

*   **Cluster Buying:** When multiple insiders (two or more) buy stock around the same time, it sends a stronger signal and suggests a consensus that the company is undervalued or on the cusp of good news. More insiders buying indicates a greater chance the news is strong.
*   **Conviction and Size:** Insiders who are putting **significant money** into a purchase are showing conviction. For this service, we prioritize trades that are substantial in dollar value, as purchases of small amounts (like $3,000‚Äì$15,000) are often routine 401k contributions or automated buys and are **not considered opportunistic**.
*   **Targeting CFOs and VPs:** We often place **more weight on trades made by the Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** and Vice Presidents than the CEO. CFOs are intimately connected to the company's financials, liabilities, and valuation, making their purchase a very strong signal of conviction.
